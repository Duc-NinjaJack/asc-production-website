Volatility clusters and changing market conditions are normal in emerging markets. ASC treats large losses as a system-design problem, not as something that can be wished away.

ASC cannot control the market or predict every downturn. Instead, the firm defines in advance what actions may occur when losses cross certain thresholds.

Diversification, position limits and total exposure caps are used to reduce dependence on any single stock, sector or theme.

When losses approach written limits, rules may call for trimming exposure or using suitable hedging tools to reduce net market risk. If stress deepens, the response can become more defensive.

These steps can lag sudden rebounds or add hedging costs. They do not promise a maximum loss level. They exist to keep risk decisions disciplined when market stress is high.
